School Days

School Days tells a frustrating and frightening tale of an harem.

School Days (2007) is a pretty horrible show, in both senses of the word. I dare not watch it again just to review or analyze it, so my memory is my only lead but that I wish I have forgotten instead. As an anime, it has horrible production and directing; as an story, it is frustrating and aggravating to watch. So to say the difficulty of watching this is high so I urge people to stay away from this despite looking like a generic school love story.

After looking back at so many anime, I have to say this anime is unique in the sense that it does what so many are afraid to do or try. People love to hate this anime, but that is the point; the characters and story are so abhorrent that you should hate it. If that was it's objective then it has done a fantastic job. My purpose is not to defend this anime but rather to understand its meaning and give me some solace in watching it.

Although I urge people to avoid this but if you care about watching it then SPOILER WARNING up ahead as I will mention the plot and more so the ending.

Summary

From the top of my head, the story goes likes this:

  • Boy has a crush on a girl
  • Female best friend helps in courting the girl
  • Boy goes in a relationship with said girl
  • Boy gets bored with girl and hooks up with best friend
  • Boy gets bored with best friend and hooks up with other friends
  • Boy gets best friend pregnant and hooks up with the first girl
  • Boy still hooks up with other friends
  • Betrayed pregnant friend kills boy
  • First girl kills pregnant friend
  • Insane first girl sleeps with the chopped head of the dead boy

In this summary the first three points are very familiar to most high school romance, the rest end in a disastrous climax. If you are genre savvy, this is indeed unique just from this plot. The main boy is pretty disgusting for getting bored and cheating on her lover. The women are questionable in themselves specially the best friend but they are motivated primarily by the consequences of the main boy. At the end the story it may be considered a good or bad ending depending on how you see it: it is a good ending if you consider it reckoning or judgment and a bad ending if you think about it traditionally as the main character dying. This is the only solace I have is that the male protagonist had it coming to him but it is still sad to see the damage done to the women around him.

Harem

I do want to direct your attention as to who the main protagonist is. His construction is that he looks generic that people can relate or project themselves into him but so atrocious that you hate to be even associated with him. Why create a contradictory main protagonist from a character writing and genre point of view? The main characteristic of a harem lead is usually just be nice or cool or lucky if that is a trait so that the women will be impressed and collected in the harem. So is it an artistic choice?

If you think about it, isn't this what men want? To be surrounded by women and dream of hooking up with them? Case in point with harem (one guy and more than two girls) shows but do not shack with them so that it maintains the tension and prolongs the competition. In this anime, the main protagonist shacks up with women in his harem thus fulfilling the male or harem dream; and if he accidentally impregnate a woman, he escapes responsibility while having more cake on the side. Isn't this what a male would want? Isn't this who are main protagonist is? The representation of the male's dream and harem? In a sense, the character construction and choice is not contradictory but indicative to the male desire and the story is merely playing it out. So in a sense this is an ideal harem; however, this is not the story or end we want.

By stressing who the main character represents, this may be considered a deconstruction of the harem genre by showing the dark end by fulfilling it. If the word deconstruction sounds pretentious, the word I use is anti-harem. Do we expect to be rewarded for being unfaithful? Do we expect to be rewarded for being insensitive and indecisive? Do we expect to be rewarded for escaping responsibilities? Harems play with these notions for comedy and fan service but if taken seriously should we really expect a happy ending? In consequence, are harems really good?

If indeed this is the goal or objective of the anime, then it has done a fantastic job. If you hated the characters, if you despised the story, if you felt dirty at the end of it; then it's doing its job of portraying the dangers of an harem. For all the anime I've watched, I have to give it praise for having balls to present such an unpleasant story even if you and I didn't enjoy it.

Closure

Is that what this anime really wanted to do or show? Regardless, it is still a bad show just from its animation and direction. With that in mind, was it just an accident that it might be an deconstruction? The intention doesn't seem to match the production so I am not very hopeful that I watched a poorly produced show. Now if this anime was made with high grade production and animation, would I still watched the show? If I knew it was an anti-harem, I might consider but I am still not a fan of the genre so the chances are slim.

Looking at what it stands, would I want my harem to be typical or grim? What I rather want of my shows is that they have meaning and value, whatever genre that might be. At the least, I would not watch shows that are poorly made although they might have meaning or mean well.
