Kimi no Na wa

Kimi no Na wa message deviates from the creator's previous works

Kimi no Na wa (2016) is one of Makoto Shinkai's beautiful work. It is visually magnificent, spectacularly directed and romantically moving. Praises and adulation, nothing less from his works and I would love to rewatch it. After noting how great it is from a viewing experience, I now want to talk about what its message and to me.

If you watched the film and any of his previous works, I urge you to consider and think about the message of this film. Do think about it even for a minute. After thinking long and hard about it, I have nothing. Nothing. The film is a great romance but what else is it? Is it about isolation or dreaming? Is it about time travel or ghosts? Is it about the underworld or dreams? I cannot see an underlying message compared to his other previous works which makes me sad. This is such a departure from what I like about his works: beautiful inside and out.

If you can find any meaningful message in this film, do shoot me an email and you can skip my nil analysis. Otherwise, let's talk about his previous work in comparison to this one.

SPOILER WARNING

Present

Let me summarize the film:

  • Boy swaps bodies with a girl periodically and mysteriously
  • The boy as the girl helps her in her life and vice-versa
  • Boy and girl get used to each other swapping
  • They stop swapping
  • Boy finds where girl lives
  • Boy discovers girl is a ghost from the past
  • Boy swaps with the girl to save her from her death
  • Boy saves her but both forget there lover's name
  • Time skip where boy and girl feels something missing
  • Boy and girl reunite with both asking there names

Again it is a nice romantic tale and nothing wrong with that. Digging deeper into this list there are varying notions that one can look up further. This is not a comprehensive breakdown but more of an impression, so I may be wrong but I don't think it will deviate from the flow

Ghosts

So the reason why they can switch bodies periodically or every other day is that the girl is a ghost: the girl possesses the body of the boy while the boy relives the memory the girl on the same day. Also they communicate using there phones via their journals and reading it the other day. Clever enough.

There are just some weird things I can ask about this such how one could translate to each others live without much fuss and why aren't they scared about it but it more of nitpick on my part which I can let slide.

Time Travel

So the girl is a ghost from the past and reliving the memories of the past. So how does the boy save the girl from the past? Time travel. By consuming a fermented wine made by the girl, he can trigger a body swap where it affects the past? I'm a little sketchy on this but I understand there is a form of recursion and has been foreshadowed by the threaded bracelet the girl makes.

I would understand he can recall the memory but not affect it but there is an emphasis on the mystical and Japanese so I can't really speak about it.

Red Thread

The boy and girl are related by a red thread of sort both physically and visually. The girl has given the boy a red threaded bracelet, the boy travels through time through a red thread, the girl has a red hair band and they meet through a red stairway. I understand the red thread of fate symbolizes there connection or love that will connect them through time.

Message

So love that is tied through time is quite romantic but is there anything else? The use of red, the string of fate, and time travel seem to just point to romance but what does the romance mean?

What if they never met each other? It would contradict the red string of fate. How is there longing portrayed after forgetting there names? Not much but several scenes hooking up the side characters. What is the meaning of the comet breaking apart? So that they can be tied to a global cosmic event.

What if there is really no message? If that is true, I fear for the next films of Shinkai for it will merely look pretty to everyone, that itself is okay. However, I go to his films because of the meaning and message you have to think and interpret.

The Place Promised in Our Early Days

Starting the list is where I am confused the most. A quick rundown of the story formula would be:

  • Boy and best bro meet girl
  • Comatose girl disappears and boys grieve over her
  • Best bro finds girl
  • Main boy finds a way to wake up the girl
  • Boy does so but girl forgets her feelings
  • Happily ever after?

Among his works, this is the first one that uses time travel or parallel worlds like our main film but it is a little more convoluted. What I want to focus more is the tower, which seems to represent the dream of the world and the theme of dreams in the film. This single solitary tower can be seen by everyone as a symbol of a place people can go and dream and share a common dream. The major plot point about this tower is that it takes the girl inside it so that the tower can tell the dreams to the girl. Yes, the tower for some reason and how and if it is alive or not takes the girl as a hostage for the boy to save. So the boys fly a plane to take the girl inside the tower and then blow it up. Convoluted.

What I find confusing about this film is that they blow up the tower and imply it is an happy ending although the feelings of the girl is lost or forgotten. If the tower represents the dream of the boy and girl and they destroyed it, does that mean there dreams are less important than being together? There is another angle of two nations being united by the destruction of the tower but the tower itself was made by one of the nations, there dream of sort. One final angle is where the world's made by the tower is the dream of the universe, on what the possibilities are. If you take the destruction of the tower as symbol for accepting reality over dreams for all parties, that for me is not a happy ending but a bittersweet one.

Of course that is my interpretation. By looking at the film as is, boy getting the girl is a good ending. But by saving her she forgets her feelings, what does that mean? Perhaps to add drama at the end. If you consider that when the girl was taken and that her dream was to tell her how he loves the main boy, then losing those feeling is an indirect consequence. However, the dream of being together is what ties there romance, by being together they are actually distant. As the bittersweet violin piece played by the girl, the symbolic ending for this film is very bittersweet. The harsh reality.

If you ask me the ending would be that they leave the girl there and leave the tower alone because to dream is what keeps everyone alive, not together.

5 Centimeters Per Second

The film that got me into his other films and is my favorite. I consider this the essential Shinkai film because it tackles isolation and relationship more in depth than the others which I think is his focal points in making these films. This film is unique that it is presented as a combination of three short stories but in essence you may consider this a three act story:

  • Showing the romance between the boy and the girl
  • Exploring the growing distance between them
  • Accepting that they can never be together and moving on

Most people consider this a sad ending but I consider this a good and cathartic ending as the final scene with the trains indicate both of them going there separate way and accepting it. I have to praise Shinkai for showing a story of romance growing distant that is succinct and heartbreaking. In contrast with the previous film, this is where accepting reality is much more positive than disheartening. By rejecting the fantasy of being together it allows them to move on, what a strong message to put. No one needs saving like in the previous film, only saving oneself from needless sorrow.

I have really nothing much to say except on how you interpret it, this is still the strongest film in his repertoire in terms of meaning. I really love how this connects emotionally and intellectually appeasing to consume which took me a long time to think about.

Children Who Chase Lost Voices

My second favorite Shinkai film in terms of his message. A quick rundown would be:

  • Girl meets boy
  • Boy dies
  • Girl grieves
  • Girl and her undercover teacher find the entrance to the underworld
  • Both journey together to revive a lost one
  • Girl decides to leave but teacher stays on the quest
  • Tying the threads up

This is a straightforward film about accepting death and grief and moving on. Nothing truly complicated about this film but it is still leaves a strong impact on me. Would you take a journey to be together with ones that have left you? I might consider it but no and it leaves me in tears that you can but it may not be the what we really want. Such is the nature of grief.

Again, nothing truly complicated but there may be references and issues you can spot and think about it further. One that comes into mind where the girl is being chased by the shadows, think what you may about despair or rape.

Garden Of Words

Another gem of a film. The usual rundown:

  • Boy meets older woman when it rains
  • Boy keeps meeting with woman
  • Boy realizes the woman is her teacher
  • Boy and teacher comes to term with there state in life
  • Boy and teacher never meetup

You may hone in on the use of light, rain and shoes signifying the truth, isolation and moving on respectively. Some people might find the fascination of the boy with making shoes a little queer and the backstory might phone in Freud but I do like this film. What stands out is the open ending, which has a greater weight on what there relationship means or stands for. The boy wants to please an older woman as how her mother does and the woman depends on a partner to be emotionally stable. Near the end, the boy makes a pair of shoe at the end but leaves it there while the woman is teaching and it never rains but snows. It implies that both have moved on from there issue but never destined to meet as it shows snow and the boy leaving the pair instead of waiting and coming back. Leaving there ending and relationship closed and open is very moving.

Of course, you can say it will rain meaning that they will meet each other but the question is what did there relationship mean? Culturally in Japan, there relationship might be odd since the man is the primary provider but here it is the older woman; if they continue there relationship would it be socially awkward and would it be proper in other context? For me there relationship stemmed from there shared isolation from the world, they grew together from it thus there relationship has no further points. I'm not saying that they should or should not meet but it the relationship in my head is consumed or fulfilled, there is no need or urgency. That is the fun thing about the meaning of there relationship, it is open for interpretation on what you think it would be. It is not exactly deep but it stays with you.

Whether they are to be or not, it has weight and meaning on the message of the film just with the previous films.

Message

So we come back to our original film and what it means. If you cut out the part after the town is saved, it is just a sad ending but since it is there it is a good one. That's the thing it is just a matter of a sad or happy, whether they get back together or not has no meaning or message. Hypothetically, if they never met up what is the film telling you? Never get mixed up with ghosts? If they did meet up what did it mean? Magic exists? So what did it mean?

That is why I think this movie is pretty but hollow. However, why should it matter it if did have a message or not when it is well made? Actually it does not, I simply wanted to point out that this is his first film where it has no meaning. For now it passes but as soon as he makes another one that has no meaning then he might head to the path of the pretty romantic film. For me, I love his films for its beauty and depth and that's why I was eagerly anticipating this film but stayed at the credits wondering what just happened. Where was the thought provoking aspect of being in a relationship?

You can say that was what he was going for and if so then well done but the use of time travel and ghosts is overkill just to have a body swapping story. I would be okay if the mechanics of body swapping were never revealed like in Midori Days, you simple accepted it as part of the story. I would be okay if this movie was about long distance relationships but the second act was simply to forget there names and resolve the mechanic leaving a hollow message on what there relationship means. If it was focused more on understanding through body swapping or having a distant relationship could be close, I might be hailing him.

Parting Words

It doesn't matter although I find this alarming. I await his next film as with Mamoru Hosoda and be amazed by there spectacular works. Again, this is a strong romantic film and I would be eager to rewatch it and maybe find the real message. Here ends the observation of a Shinkai fan.
